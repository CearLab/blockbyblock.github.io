About Block by Block workshop

Construction robotics represents a significant leap forward in the building industry, where autonomous robots both prepare construction sites by pushing, moving, and organizing loose materials such as soil and gravel, and assemble structures by placing modular prefabricated building elements with precision. In this workshop, we delve into the key aspects of this emerging field, focusing on the unique challenges and opportunities presented by multi-agent systems (MAS) in modular construction and push manipulation. We begin by exploring the fundamentals of construction robotics, focusing on how robots autonomously perform a range of building tasks. Modular construction, where prefabricated units are assembled into larger structures, offers a flexible, scalable approach ideal for robotic systems. Coordinating a team of heterogeneous robots to efficiently execute these tasks requires sophisticated planning and scheduling algorithms. Conversely, teams composed of independent agents each with limited information must coordinate their actions without the benefit of a central omniscient planner. The workshop will address sequencing block placements and ensuring seamless coordination among agents, for both centralized and decentralized systems.

Additionally, we will examine how robots with generic pushing tools can be organized to shape and manipulate materials solely through pushing actions. This method leverages the versatility of pushing tasks for site preparation, enabling robots to arrange and contour materials with precision before assembly begins. Strategies for coordinating these actions among multiple robots will be discussed, optimizing the groundwork for modular construction.

This workshop aims to equip participants with the knowledge and tools needed to advance the field of construction robotics, emphasizing the practical and theoretical aspects of MAS coordination in this dynamic environment.
